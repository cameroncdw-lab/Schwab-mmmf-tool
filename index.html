All yields are already net of expense ratio — the 7-day SEC yield and 30-day SEC yield standards subtract fees before publication. The exp ratio column is shown for transparency only; you don't need to subtract it yourself.

No transaction fees: All Schwab Money Funds have no transaction fees. SGOV is an iShares (BlackRock) ETF — ETFs generally trade commission-free on Schwab, but verify for your specific account type.

SGOV price stability: Unlike a money market mutual fund's guaranteed $1.00 NAV, SGOV's price fluctuates slightly (~$100/share, 52-week range $100.27–$100.74). In practice this is negligible for a cash reserve position, but it is technically not a stable NAV product.

TEQ (tax-equivalent yield) = yield ÷ (1 − federal rate). This is what a fully taxable fund must yield to match after-tax. Shown for fed-exempt muni funds and SGOV (state exempt). SWKXX is only relevant for California clients. Ultra shares require $1M minimum.

Updating yields: Visit schwabassetmanagement.com/products/money-fund-yields and update the TAXABLE_FUNDS, EXEMPT_FUNDS, and ETF_FUNDS arrays in the script. For SGOV use the 30-day SEC yield from iShares.com. Change LAST_UPDATED to the new date.
